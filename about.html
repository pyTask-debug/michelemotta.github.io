Background

No, I'm not the kind of person who writes about their grandfather being a doctor, their first stethoscope at age four, and things like that. I'm still figuring out what I want my "final" field of work to be. Everything I know about what I like, I've learned over time.

I grew up as a very emotional kid, and when the time came to choose a university path, I combined my emotional side with another interest that developed during high school: science — especially mathematics, even though I wasn't very good at it. My interest in the brain — needless to say — is the ultimate combination of psychology (that is, behavior) and biology. In the past couple of years, I've inevitably come into contact with programming, which, I have to admit, I find really fun.

Beyond Science

Even though science takes up a lot of personal time (although I believe it should be a job that requires as many hours as any other), a researcher is not just a researcher — they are also a person with hobbies and interests.

I am very passionate about politics, I read a lot, and I love cycling up in the mountains.
